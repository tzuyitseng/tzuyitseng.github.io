I am a cognitive-neurolinguist, interested in how language is represented in the human brain and mind across languages. My research focuses on understanding how speech sounds, word meanings and emotional cues are encoded and decoded during speech processing, and how our native language influences the way we hear, understand, and learn a foreign language.


Along my research journey, I explored semantic and phonological associations in the mental lexicon during my MA in Linguistics (National Chengchi University, Taipei, Taiwan, 2020). Then, I studied sensorimotor representations for native and non-native phoneme perception during my PhD in Neuroscience (Université Lumière Lyon 2, Lyon, France, January 2025). Through my research in psycho- and neurolinguistics, particularly in speech production and perception, I have developed expertise in:
  • fMRI experiments (3T sparse and continuous sequence)
  • Multi-voxel pattern analysis (Nilearn/scikit-learn/rsatoolbox)
  • Statistical analysis/programming (Python, R)
  • Speech processing (Praat)
In the next phase of my journey, I aim to extend my research to explore how language is represented in neurodivergent populations, while also refining my methodological toolkit through advanced neuroimaging techniques (such as 7T fMRI and M/EEG). These tools will enhance my investigation of the temporal and spatial dynamics of language processing. I'm always open to discussions and I welcome academic collaborations, especially within (but not limited to) my current research interests in cognitive psycho- and neurolinguistics with cross-linguistic comparison for:
  • Sensorimotor representations in language processing
  • Speech processing of articulatory features
  • Prosodic encoding of emotions
  • Structure and access of the mental lexicon

Feel free to reach out to me for brainstorming research ideas!
